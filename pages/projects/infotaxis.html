Overview

Plant pests and diseases cause significant losses in agricultural and horticultural crops, reducing global potential production by up to 40% annually. Currently, these threats are managed through widespread chemical treatments, which pose serious environmental risks, including groundwater contamination and harm to wildlife, particularly pollinators, and potentially humans. Ensuring effective plant health management is essential for food security, and mitigating negative impacts requires precise, localized application of control measures. This project aims to develop algorithms for detecting and localizing infection hotspots through olfactory sensing of chemical markers associated with specific pests and diseases. Autonomous systems, equipped with specialized sensors and guided by mathematical olfactory search algorithms, will perform this task.
